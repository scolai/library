what's a good way to organize working titles for a newsletter

Organizing working titles for a newsletter can be a creative playground—here’s a system that keeps things tidy *and* keeps inspiration flowing:

---

### 🗂️ **Title Tracking System for Newsletters**

**1. Create a Master Title List (Spreadsheet or Notion Table)**  
Columns could include:
- **Working Title**
- **Theme/Topic**
- **Date Scheduled or Published**
- **Audience Hook or Angle**
- **Status** (e.g., Idea, Drafting, Ready, Sent)

**2. Use Categories or Tags**  
Group titles under recurring segments like:
- Destinations (e.g., “Bali on a Budget”)
- Tips/How-Tos (“Pack Smarter: 5 Hacks from a Flight Attendant”)
- Stories (“That One Time in Lisbon…”)
- Deals & Updates (“Flight Deals Under $400 This Week”)

**3. Keep an “Idea Dump” Section**  
Drop in raw title ideas as they come—snippets, half-thoughts, puns. It’s your creative sandbox.

**4. Color Code by Priority or Timing**  
Visually track what's evergreen vs. seasonal, urgent vs. filler.

**5. Review & Refine**  
Come back weekly or monthly to tighten, combine, or queue up your best performers.
