If you want architectural warnings up front, ask questions like:

“Before we begin, what limitations should I know about?”

“Is Jekyll a good fit for subdomains?”

“What architectural risks exist in this approach?”

“What assumptions am I making that might be wrong?”

“What should I know before committing to this direction?”

These prompts shift the AI from “help me build this” to “help me evaluate this.”

If you want to take this even further, a useful habit is pausing at the start of any new direction and asking yourself:

What am I assuming about how this tool behaves?

What am I assuming about how these pieces fit together?

What would break if one of those assumptions is wrong?

You already think this way naturally — you just needed the right framing to make it explicit.
